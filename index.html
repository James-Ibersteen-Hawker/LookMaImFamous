Early Life

Ferdinand Zeppelin was born in Zepelin, Germany, on July 8th 1838, son of Württemberg Minister and Hofmarschall Friedrich Jerôme Wilhelm Karl Graf von Zeppelin and his wife Amélie Françoise Pauline. He grew up at their manor near Konstanz, living with his brother and sister, and taught by private tutors. In 1853, Zeppelin went to attend the polytechnic institute in Stuttgart, and joined the military at Ludwigsburg in 1855.

Later Life

In 1863, Zeppelin went to Minnesota, US, where he worked as an airborne balloon spotter for the Union Troops during the Civil War. He fought in the Seven Weeks War and in the Franco-German War, being a member of Prussia’s and Germany’s armies. After 1890, he retired from military service and dedicated his life to rigid airship invention and development. After 10 years of failed attempts, he finally created the LZ-1, which flew on July 2 1900. The German government then ordered a fleet, which were used widely throughout WWI. Zeppelin married Isabella Freiin von Wolff, with whom he had a daughter, Helene.

Contributions to the World

  1. Airships were a fairly large part of Germany’s WWI airborne arsenal.
  2. Airships were used quite often for the purposes of long voyages, as they could hold more people than any airplane of the day and could travel farther and in a much higher degree of comfort.
  3. Count Zeppelin created the first airship airline.
  4. Count Zeppelin pioneered lighter-than-air mass transit and large lighter-than-air craft.
